Aydın Yelkovan

for Executive Vice President of External Affairs

I’m Aydın, a third-year Political Science transfer running for EVP of External Affairs to end the model of academia that treats students as customers rather than citizens and education as a commodity rather than a right. I will nourish and channel our aspirational voice into actionable public policy.

As I gathered endorsements for ballot access, I spoke with 300+ of you about your vision. I heard excellent ideas: free parking, 24-hour restaurants and markets, expanded housing options, pedagogy training for professors, bikes for intracampus mobility, specialized disability accommodations, and more.

The truth is simple: UC has more than enough money for this; it’s allocated wrong. We have $1.4 billion invested in Blackstone, the world’s largest commercial landlord, even as students pay about $1,300 a month and still get stuck in triples. We should divest from practices that threaten our future and invest in affordability: end parking enforcement quotas, expand free or discounted student parking, and fund Rapid Rehousing.

Unelected Regents are insulated from us. As a mandate on accountability, I’ll establish the UC Students’ Assembly: an annual summit where a lottery-selected sample of students deliberate on critical issues directly with administration. Plus, the Student’s Initiative—enough signatures to put policy on the ballot, with paid petition circulation prohibited.

Our professors are great researchers, not always great teachers. Mandate pedagogy training for all professors. UCSD expands class sizes and cuts TA lines, which limits support for students. I organize with UAW 4811 across graduate workers and undergrad partners: building solidarity, expanding the Immigrant Legal Defense Fund, and pushing for more TA appointments alongside stronger outcomes for students.

What I’ve worked on:

I’m rooted in Bay Area, San Diego, and state politics—electoral and campaign finance reform, climate, tenant protections, school transit, and divestment—and I work with the Students’ Civil Liberties Union (SCLU) on ICE accountability and sanctuary organizing, disability justice on campus, and connecting San Diego campaigns to partners statewide. I collaborate with public officials, community organizations, and constituents in those fights. I moved from the East Bay last fall and plugged into housing, immigrant, disability, and climate justice here.

In the East Bay I was Events Coordinator for Students for Justice in Palestine at Diablo Valley College—recognized with “Best Cultural Impact” and a hand in drafting the Northern California SJP Coalition Constitution. With RepresentUs I served as Call Captain and Local Leader, building messaging against corporate money in politics and for ranked-choice voting. As a lobbyist with California Clean Money Campaign, I helped qualify the Fair Elections Act for the 2026 ballot. I also advised Ethical Investing for Concord, advancing a municipal ethical investment policy.

In San Diego I’ve led SCLU canvassing for sanctuary spaces; co-launched the Disability Resource Hub with Blind Snakes Co-Operative and tied it to disability studies across the UC; built community trainings to track ICE activity before raids; with Ellis Collective pushed UCSDPD to reject DHS grants for AI surveillance and worked with faculty to move off invasive e-proctoring; and with SJP San Diego helped oppose the hiring of military strategist Eitan Shamir. I’ve organized with DSA, ACCE, the Tenants’ Council of San Diego, The Justice Workshop, and other local partners.

EVP of External Affairs is the external advocate for undergrads, representing us through the UC Student Association, US Student Association, and advocacy in government and civic society, and internally within the AS Senate.

Nothing motivates me more than building a world that affords everyone the dignified opportunity to reach our full potential. Let’s build together.

Vote AYE on Aydın and rank me first on your ballot. A vote for me is a vote for every community this university has failed to protect — for the movements already fighting for you.
