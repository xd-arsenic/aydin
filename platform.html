Platform

I’m running on equity, empowerment, engagement, and experience.

  • Equity: Invest in students’ basic needs and end paywalls to public education.
  • Empowerment: Transition Associated Students into an independent, democratic student union with easier mechanisms for direct student engagement.
  • Engagement: Build academic standards that make abstract course concepts tangible, collaborative, engaging, and relevant.
  • Experience: Energize campus with 24-hour markets, a bike-share program, and creative spaces.

EQUITY: Affordability

Parking
Designate 15 free 2-hour parking spots in every parking structure. End parking enforcement quotas.
Housing
Expand the funding and scope of the Student Rapid Rehousing program.
Groceries
Ensure affordable food options are available in campus markets.
Divestment from unsustainable practices
Adopt an Ethical Investment Policy to divest from harmful practices and free up funds for investments in students’ basic needs and sustainable technologies. Amend the current University of California Investment Policy to prohibit investment in funds whose investments exceed a threshold of total holdings in firms who enable, or directly profit from any of the following: fossil fuel extraction and processing, arms manufacturing, the production and sale of tobacco or weaponry, prison or immigrant detention facilities, mass surveillance technologies, or firms complicit in severe and consistent human rights violations, including slave labor, prison labor, war crimes, genocide, ethnic cleansing, apartheid.

EMPOWERMENT: Democracy

ASUCSD independence (501(c)(3))
Transition ASUCSD into an independent non-profit, ending the administrative veto on Associated Students’ decisions. By transitioning ASUCSD into a 501(c)(3) non-profit, we will grant students complete autonomy in running student-run spaces and programs, challenging administrative decisions, and partnering with external community organizations, such as labor unions. The movement toward ASUCSD independence will be a UC-wide solidarity movement, where we join with other campuses to overturn the policy that reduces our democratic student government into a unit of the university.
UC Students’ Assembly
Establish and annualize the UC Students’ Assembly, a summit where a lottery-selected sample of students are invited to deliberate on critical issues directly with UC Regents, Chancellors, and other key stakeholders.
UCSD Students’ Initiative
Establish the UCSD Students’ Initiative, allowing students to propose policy directly on the ballot upon collection of required signatures, with paid circulation prohibited and a signature threshold of 15% of total undergraduate turnout from the most recent regularly scheduled election.
Transparency on the AS website
Ensure publicly elected representatives’ emails are made available on the Associated Students website so students and stakeholders can reach out. Currently, the ASUCSD Senate hosts its most up-to-date information on social media pages, while the authoritative website lags behind. Consequently, none of the AS Senate representatives have pictures or email addresses posted.
Time, place, and manner (free speech)
Defend First Amendment rights by amending the Time, Place, Manner policies to enshrine the free speech rights of students and workers on-campus. These restrictive policies are used to repress student and worker activism, limiting free speech to tiny so-called “free speech zones” and militantly enforcing against violators. They are also blatant violations of First Amendment jurisprudence which should ensure TPM designation does not interfere with the ability of the petitioners to reach their intended audience.
Understudy program for new electeds
Encourage candidacy by creating an understudy program, where new ASUCSD electeds shadow the operations of their office and are onboarded. Many of the seats went unopposed this election cycle.

ENGAGEMENT: Quality education

Pedagogy training
Mandate pedagogy training for professors, emphasizing teaching methods that ensure course concepts are actionable, relevant, and accessible to students.
TA-to-student ratios
Cap TA-to-student ratios to prevent overwork of teaching assistants, ensuring they can provide the necessary support and resources students deserve.
Disability accommodations
End quarterly re-verification of disabilities.

EXPERIENCE: Campus life

24-hour markets
Expand market hours to 24 hours per day to accommodate the diverse schedules of students, aligning our infrastructure with the reality of a 24/7 academic environment. We already have cashier-less systems, so this is a low-cost expansion.
Bike-share program
Establish a university-owned bike-share program, building bike hubs where students can unlock bikes and helmets with smart locks via Tap-to-Ride with their student ID.
Creative spaces
Support creative spaces on-campus! Sciences and arts are only useful once applied — let’s preserve and expand spaces where students can apply their learning collaboratively and accessibly.
